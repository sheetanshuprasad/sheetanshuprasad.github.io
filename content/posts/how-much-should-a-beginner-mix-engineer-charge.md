One of the most common questions new mix engineers ask is how much they should charge for their services.

Many beginners either charge too little or too much. Finding the right balance is important for attracting clients while still valuing your time and skills.

## Avoid Working for Free Forever

Offering a few free mixes to build a portfolio can be useful. However, continuing to work for free for months or years often leads to burnout and attracts clients who do not value your work.

The goal should be to move toward paid projects as soon as possible.

## Start with Beginner-Friendly Rates

If you are just starting and have little client experience, consider charging modest rates.

For example:

- Single song mix: $20-$50
- EP project: discounted package pricing
- Additional revisions: small extra fee

The exact amount depends on your skills, experience, and local market.

## Focus on Building Trust

Clients are not only paying for a mix.

They are paying for:

- Communication
- Reliability
- Meeting deadlines
- Professionalism

A positive client experience often matters as much as technical ability.

## Raise Prices Gradually

As your portfolio grows and you gain testimonials, increase your rates.

A common mistake is keeping beginner prices for too long.

If clients consistently accept your quotes without hesitation, it may be a sign that your rates are too low.

## Create Packages

Instead of offering only one service, consider creating packages.

Examples:

- Basic Mix
- Mix + Master
- Mix + Master + Revisions

Packages make it easier for clients to choose and can increase project value.

### Final Thoughts

There is no perfect price for every beginner mix engineer.

Start with reasonable rates, focus on delivering excellent service, and gradually increase your prices as your experience and reputation grow.
